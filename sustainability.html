Our Sustainability Framework

Nestled in one of the world's most fragile marine ecosystems, The Riviera operates with the understanding that our island is not ours to own — it is ours to protect. We have aligned our operations with five United Nations Sustainable Development Goals that are most relevant to the Andaman archipelago, committing to measurable action across every aspect of resort life.

SDG Goal 14

SDG Goal 14  ·  Life Below Water

Guardians of
the Reef

Why this matters at The Riviera

The Andaman Islands host some of the most biodiverse coral reefs in the Indian Ocean — and some of the most threatened. As a private island resort, our primary amenity is not our villas; it is the ocean. We treat it accordingly. Every decision we make, from operations to guest experiences, is filtered through one question: does this protect what lives beneath the waves?

  • Coral Outplanting Programme: Our marine biologists grow coral fragments in underwater nurseries and transplant them to degraded reef sections, restoring over 500 m² of reef since 2023.
  • Plastic-Free Island Policy: Zero single-use plastics on the entire property. All guest amenities use glass, bamboo, or compostable alternatives.
  • Marine "No-Take" Zone: The 2 km radius around the island is designated a protected no-fishing, no-anchoring zone, allowing fish populations to recover naturally.
  • Guest Conservation Dives: Guests can join weekly reef monitoring dives led by our resident marine biologist, turning tourism into active conservation.
SDG Goal 12

SDG Goal 12  ·  Responsible Consumption & Production

Wasting Nothing,
Sourcing Wisely

Why this matters at The Riviera

Supplying a private island resort in the Andamans is logistically challenging — every item shipped from the mainland carries a carbon cost. This reality pushes us to be radical about local sourcing and waste reduction. Our goal is simple: everything that enters the island either came from a nearby community or leaves the island as a resource, never as rubbish.

  • Farm-to-Table Sourcing: All seafood is sourced directly from Port Blair's licensed fishermen cooperatives. Seasonal vegetables are grown on our island kitchen garden, supplemented by Andamanese farmers.
  • On-Site Glass Bottling Plant: We purify and bottle our own drinking water in reusable glass bottles, eliminating an estimated 80,000 plastic bottles per year.
  • 100% Organic Waste Composting: All kitchen and garden waste is converted into compost through our on-site biodigester, which also feeds back into our landscaping cycle.
  • Linen & Amenity Lifecycle: Towels and linens are replaced only on guest request. Unused toiletries are donated to local schools and communities in Port Blair.
SDG Goal 7

SDG Goal 7  ·  Affordable & Clean Energy

Powered by
Sun & Sea

Why this matters at The Riviera

Most islands in the Andamans run on diesel generators — expensive, noisy, and deeply polluting. The Riviera made the decision at construction stage to build differently. By generating our own clean energy and designing our architecture to work with the tropical climate instead of fighting it, we aim to be the first diesel-free luxury resort in the Andaman archipelago.

  • Island Solar Microgrid: A 450 kW rooftop and ground-mounted solar array powers all guest villas, resort facilities, and our desalination plant, with a battery bank for overnight and cloudy-day storage.
  • Passive Cooling Architecture: All villas are oriented to capture the prevailing south-west sea breeze. Deep overhanging eaves, high ceilings, and shading screens dramatically reduce air-conditioning load.
  • Solar Water Heating: Vacuum-tube solar collectors provide 100% of domestic hot water, with zero reliance on electric heaters.
  • EV Island Buggies: All on-island transport uses solar-charged electric vehicles, eliminating exhaust emissions from the island entirely.
SDG Goal 8

SDG Goal 8  ·  Decent Work & Economic Growth

Empowering
Island Communities

Why this matters at The Riviera

Sustainable tourism is hollow if the community next door does not benefit. The Andaman Islands have a young, talented population that has historically had limited access to world-class hospitality careers. The Riviera was designed to change this — not through charity, but through genuine employment, fair pay, and professional investment in local people.

  • Local-First Hiring Policy: A minimum of 85% of all positions — from chefs to guest relations managers — are filled by residents of Port Blair and the Andaman & Nicobar Islands. No exceptions for mid-level and below.
  • The Riviera Hospitality Academy: A 6-month accredited training programme for local youth, covering culinary arts, front office management, English communication, and international hospitality standards.
  • Artisan Partnership Programme: We commission local woodcarvers, weavers, and potters to supply resort décor, ensuring traditional crafts generate sustainable income for island artisans.
  • Living Wage Standard: All staff, including seasonal and contracted workers, are paid a verified living wage — above the national minimum — with full provident fund and health insurance coverage.
SDG Goal 6

SDG Goal 6  ·  Clean Water & Sanitation

Pure Water,
Protected Reefs

Why this matters at The Riviera

Freshwater is the most precious and scarcest resource on a small island. Traditional groundwater extraction causes saltwater intrusion, destroying the island's natural water table forever. Equally critical: any untreated waste that reaches the ocean triggers algal blooms that suffocate coral. At The Riviera, not a single drop of groundwater is extracted, and not a single litre of untreated water leaves the island.

  • Solar-Powered Desalination: Our reverse-osmosis desalination plant, powered entirely by our solar grid, converts seawater into safe drinking water — producing 40,000 litres daily with zero impact on the island's groundwater.
  • Greywater Recycling System: A dual-pipe system collects water from all showers, sinks, and laundry, treats it through a bio-filter garden, and reuses it for 100% of the resort's landscape irrigation.
  • Zero-Liquid Discharge (ZLD) Plant: Our advanced sewage treatment plant processes all wastewater to tertiary standards. Absolutely zero liquid waste or chemicals are discharged into the surrounding ocean — ever.
  • Rainwater Harvesting: Every roof on the island is fitted with rainwater collection channels feeding into a 200,000-litre underground cistern, reducing desalination load during the monsoon season by up to 40%.
Our Commitment

Beyond Carbon Neutral
by 2030

The Riviera has committed to achieving carbon negativity by 2030 — meaning we will remove more carbon from the atmosphere than we produce. This is not an aspiration. It is a legally binding target embedded in our operating charter, verified annually by an independent environmental auditor. The ocean gave us everything. We intend to give back more.
